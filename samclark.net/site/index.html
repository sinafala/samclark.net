2023-11-16 ⇧

Move to Brazil and Fall 2023

In September, Clarissa, Oliver, and Sam moved to Brazil for most of the coming year. Sam has a year-long sabbatical, and both Clarissa and Sam have a project in Brazil funded by the Gates foundation. We are creating a 'reference death archive' for verbal autopsy at the WHO in Geneva. The Department of Pathology at the University of São Paulo runs the autopsy service for the City of São Paulo and will be producing many of the reference deaths that will go to the archive, and we are getting that going now.

Sam just finished a ridiculous round of traveling that started at the beginning of September and ended over the past weekend: Columbus → Washington DC → Columbus → Princeton → Columbus → Brazil → Columbus → Kenya → South Africa → Germany → Columbus → Brazil → Switzerland, and finally → Brazil! That was not fun, but lots of useful work and a move to Brazil happened!

Oliver is enrolled in high school in Araucária where we are living with Clarissa's parents on a small farm by a lake. He's enjoying it, and we're enjoying the place too! We bought a tiny Suzuki Jimny 4x4 which is suitable for tight spaces, slow/rough roads, and getting to remote trail heads in the mountains. It's also very fuel efficient. We've driven it to the beach a few times where Clarissa's parents have a small house, and Oliver and I have taken it up in the mountains to do some hiking.

Paula is about to finish Penn! She will graduate in December with a degree in sociology and a specialization in education. She wants to work in education starting as a teacher, and she has applied for three positions; is interviewing for two of them; waiting for response on the third. She's also applying to graduate school. We'll see what happens!

Sophia applied for and was accepted to a new post at Miller-Knoll where she has been working since graduation. She is now part of the marketing team with significantly more responsibility and a much more varied set of things to do. She's enjoying that and the significant boost to the bottom line that it came with.

Everyone - including Wilfred - will be here in Brazil for the end of the year, and since Paula is graduating a semester early, she'll stay with us here until early summer 2024. We're looking forward to all of that!

2023-08-01 ⇧

Sam Infection

In early June, Sam very quickly (over the course of one day!) developed a serious fever with a generalized feeling of being unwell and specific pain in the lower abdomen. A quick visit to the clinic resulted in hospitalization with an uncharacterized infection. Over the course of the next few days, it was localized to the prostate and identified as a very serious, multi-drug resistant strain of E. Coli that can only be acquired in a hospital setting - most likely a routine, preventative colonoscopy several years ago! There exists only one really effective drug to treat this and two less-effective options. After getting permission from the powers that be, Sam started the effective drug as a daily at-home IV infusion for six weeks at about $700 per dose! The drug worked but had uncomfortable side effects, so Sam didn't do much but sleep and read for six weeks while that was going on.

Namibia: Sam and Clarissa

We were married on June 13, 1992. So our 30-year anniversary was June 13, 2022. We had planned a nice trip for ourselves last year, but a variety of things eventually made it impossible, so we postponed. During spring break a week ago, we went to Namibia to celebrate our 30-year anniversary and relax. It was fantastic! Sam first went to Namibia in 1989 to teach calculus at the University of Namibia during the summer. He returned in 1998 for fun. Clarissa had never been there. Since his first trip, Namibia has been Sam's favourite African country, and it remains that way!

2022-32-24 ⇧

Clarks: Best of 2022 by Sophia

Happy Holidays!

This year the Clarks traveled far and wide and will spend the end-of-year in Southern Brazil – the first time all of us have been in Brazil since the mid-2000s!

As for the kids –

Paula is a junior at the University of Pennsylvania where she studies sociology and education. This year she decided she wants to get a Master’s degree in teaching, and she has spent much of the year figuring out where and how she can get the degree after she graduates from Penn in the winter of 2023. She was recently elected co-chair of the West Philadelphia Tutoring Project, a student-led club with over 90 Penn students who tutor K-32 students in reading, writing and math. She continues to enjoy her apartment in West Philadelphia where she replaced the plants Sophia lent her last year with her own plant jungle.

In line with her interest in biking and outdoor activities, Paula was a bike tour leader in Nova Scotia for Overland. She, along with three other leaders, led 20 high school students through Maine and Nova Scotia.

Oliver is a sophomore at St. Francis DeSales High School, where along with his studies, he has joined many clubs including environmental, drone and engineering. All save the drone club were a letdown. Most recently he started a snow-blowing business in the neighborhood - his most successful business to date. Oliver visited Paula in Philadelphia in October on his first solo trip.

This summer, Oliver and Sam undertook their pilgrimage (i.e. mountain bike trip) West and explored mountain bike trails in Utah, Arizona and Colorado - see mountain bike page.

Injuries sustained:
  • Sam: sprained ankle with a recovery time of six months
  • Oliver: sprained wrist from a bad crash, recovery time of two weeks

Sophia started the year by buying her first car - a blue Subaru Outback Wilderness - a much-anticipated milestone. She completed her first sprint triathlon in May shortly before road tripping across the country to her new home city of Oakland, California. In July, she and her boyfriend Wilfred moved into an apartment in Rockridge. She spent the remainder of the year settling into the apartment, meeting fellow Bay Area triathletes, completing a second triathlon, this time an aquathon, which is the Olympic distance swim and bike ride of a triathlon.

One of Sophia’s goals for 2022 was to travel to 10 national parks. She fell short of that goal but managed to travel to six by the end of the year (Cuyahoga Falls National Park, Shenandoah National Park, Pinnacoles National Park, Kings Canyon and Sequoia National Parks, Yosemite National Park). She completed many hikes and backpacking trips in Ohio and California.

Paula visited the Bay area twice in the latter half of the year and the entire family spent Thanksgiving just north of San Francisco in Shelter Cove, where they visited Humbolt Redwoods National Park and communed with the ancient Coastal Redwoods.

Turning to parents -

Both parents' travel schedules picked up this year, with both spending stretches of time in Brazil and West Africa on trips related to their verbal autopsy work for the Gates Foundation and the WHO. In conjunction with teaching eight classes at OSU, Clarissa traveled to Guinea Bissau for the first time in 2022, and returned three more times, to assist the Ministry of Health to implement a vital statistics system for the country. The linguist in her was delighted to begin learning Guinnean Portuguese Creole. In typical fashion, she enjoyed bringing back locally-made textiles and ceramics.

During her down time, Clarissa enjoys taking part in the local Buy Nothing group, where she donates items she acquires from Ohio auctions. In December, she received word that she is officially certified nationwide as a Foreign Language Interpreter for Portuguese by the Supreme Court of Ohio.

Sam’s year was highlighted by his bike trip with Oliver, where along with biking, they spent time 'fishing and hurting'. In addition to the previously mentioned injuries, Sam spent a portion of the year dealing with a chronic gastrointestinal illness and had emergency tooth surgery in August while on a work trip to Brazil. Workwise, Sam spent a lot of time working on verbal autopsy methods and implementation - software development and training. He has a new grant from the Gates Foundation to build a reference death repository at the WHO to help improve verbal autopsy globally. He and Clarissa will work closely with colleagues in Brazil and Africa to create the initial reference deaths.

Dog and home -

Hilde graduated from the simplest dog puzzle to more advanced dog puzzles. The house is doing well. The leaves on the trees keep falling and the mulch is mulched right on schedule in the spring. Life is good.

Wrap up -

The Clark family started the year on a hike at Mohican State Park, Ohio and hope to welcome in 2023 on the beaches in Southern Brazil.

Many thanks to all of the heart-warming people we met throughout the year. Wishing you all health and happiness.

Lots of love, the Clarks
