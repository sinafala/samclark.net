2025-12-12 ▲

Presentation with full RDA Team to launch the completed first version of the Reference Data Archive (RDA). [The linked .pptx file is password protected - push the 'Read Only' button when Powerpoint asks for a password.]

Over the past three years, I have led the creation of the RDA at WHO headquarter in Geneva. The RDA is a linked trusted researcher environment and data catalogue/repository designed to host sensitive data in any form, allow vetted researchers to access and analyze the data, and provide an easy-to-use and fully functional way to share both data and analytical results created with the data. Data and results shared using the RDA require acknowledgement of data providers and analysts and come with a permanent WHO-linked DOI so that they can always be located and cited.

The motivating idea behind the RDA is to provide meaningful reference datasets for training and validating methods to improve cause of death ascertainment. However, the scope is widening to include a variety of cause of death and mortality data managed and shared by WHO. Consequently, the RDA is likely to support a range of mortality-related work at WHO.
